Hi, my name is Wendy and I have some advice for you!

ACKNOWLEDGE IMPOSTER SYNDROME (then kick it to the curb!)

When you're a newbie, it's easy to listen to that little voice in the back of your mind that tells you that it's too hard, you can't do this, you'll never get a job, blah, blah, blah.

Sometimes it's a niggling doubt, other times a screaming howler monkey!

The way to become an expert is to keep at it, try new things, make mistakes, ask for help. Albert Einstein struggled with math as a schoolboy and look how he turned out!

DO THE WORK!

Learning a new language is challenging and you need to practice, practice, rinse and repeat EVERY single day until you become fluent.

We all lead busy lives and balancing work, family, school (or all of the above) can be a real challenge. I've found that if I go more than a day without doing some kind of coding practice, rereading class notes, listening to a podcast etc., then when it's time to work on a project, it's a struggle.

You can do this!
